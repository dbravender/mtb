  Twenty in particular is worth a hard look — it's modern, TypeScript-based, has a great UI, and is actively maintained.

  Questions to Think Through

  Before building from scratch, a few things worth answering:

  1. Which parts of HubSpot do you actually use? CRM? Email marketing? Sales pipeline? Landing pages? All of it? Narrowing scope is critical.
  2. How many contacts/users are we talking about? This affects architecture choices significantly.
  3. Is this for your own business, or a product you'd sell? Very different design constraints.
  4. Maintenance reality check — HubSpot has hundreds of engineers. A from-scratch build means you own every bug, every email deliverability issue, every security patch. Are you prepared for
  that ongoing cost?
  5. Would self-hosting an existing open-source CRM (like Twenty) + customizing it get you 80% of the way there for a fraction of the effort?

  I'm happy to help you build something if that's the right path — but I'd hate for you to spend weeks building what already exists. What are your thoughts?
